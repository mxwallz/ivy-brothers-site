Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

When should we start working with Ivy Brothers?

The earlier the better. Students who begin in 9th or 10th grade have the most opportunity to build a compelling profile, pursue selective programs, and develop the narrative that top schools respond to. That said, we do work with 11th and 12th graders and have a strong track record helping students who come to us later in the process.

What makes Ivy Brothers different from a standard college counselor?

Most college counselors advise from the outside. Our team includes former admissions officers from Harvard, Princeton, and other top schools who have actually reviewed and voted on applications. That perspective changes everything, from how we read your student's profile to how we coach their essays and interviews.

What does the admissions guarantee cover?

Our admissions guarantee reflects our commitment to delivering a quality outcome for every student we work with. The specifics are discussed during your initial consultation, as the terms depend on the student's profile, target schools, and program tier. We only offer guarantees to students we genuinely believe we can help, which is part of why our acceptance rate is as strong as it is.

How many students do you work with at once?

We intentionally limit cohort size so that every student receives genuine attention and not a template. Our advisors carry a small number of active students at any time, which is part of what makes our process work and why we review every application before accepting new clients.

Do you work with international students?

Yes. We work with students from across the US and internationally. Our advisors have experience navigating the specific considerations international applicants face, from TOEFL requirements to demonstrating ties to home country versus intent to stay, and we tailor the strategy accordingly.
